# Weekly Cycle — Edu's Meal Prep Operation
### Detailed Summary | April 2026

---

## What This Business Is

**"HelloFresh Local"** — a weekly freezer-stocking service run by Eduardo Cano Galeano (Edu),
22-year-old IGA-trained chef operating out of Lambaré, Paraguay.

Every week Edu cooks 3 dishes in bulk (15–25 portions each), vacuum-seals them, and delivers
door-to-door Sunday morning. Clients pick up their order from their own freezer and reheat in
10–15 min when needed. Each portion lasts 7 days in the fridge or 30 days in the freezer.

**Not** a personal chef. **Not** a catering service. **Not** hot delivery.
Artisan quality, freezer-stable, subscription pricing.

---

## The 7-Day Loop

```
SUN 20:00   MON–WED     WED 20:00    THU 5am     FRI          SAT 9am      SUN 9am
  Post     ← Orders →    Cutoff      Shop        Prep         Cook         Deliver
  menu                  Consolidate             Mise en place Batch        Re-post
```

One cycle = 7 days, Sunday to Sunday. Total active hours: **~28–30 hrs/week**.

## Day-by-Day Schedule

### Sunday — Publication (0.5 hrs)

| Time | Action |
|---|---|
| 20:00 | Publish weekly menu via WhatsApp group + Instagram story |
| | 2–3 options + 1 extra (soup / broth / jar) |
| | Include: photo, price, availability, order deadline |

**Output:** Menu live. Cycle opens.

---

### Monday–Wednesday — Order Collection (1.5 hrs total)

| | |
|---|---|
| Mon–Wed | Clients pre-order by WhatsApp or form |
| Passive load | ~30 min/day replying to messages |
| **Wed 20:00** | **Hard cutoff. No orders accepted after this.** |

**Rule:** If fewer than 10 portions confirmed by Wednesday 20:00 → cancel the week with notice.
Cooking below 10 portions is a loss.

---

### Wednesday Night — Consolidation (1.5 hrs)

| Task | Time |
|---|---|
| Tally all orders into spreadsheet | 30 min |
| Calculate quantities per dish × portion × ingredient factor | 30 min |
| Build shopping list per supplier | 30 min |
| Message butcher + vegetable supplier on WhatsApp | 30 min |

**Output:** Shopping list ready. Suppliers notified.

---

### Thursday — Shopping & Receiving (6 hrs)

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 5:00 | Leave for Mercado de Abasto |
| 5:30–8:00 | Buy vegetables, fruit, dry goods |
| 8:00–9:30 | Butcher / frigorífico (or receive pre-ordered bulk) |
| 9:30–11:30 | Receive, wash, refrigerate, light mise en place |

**Shopping protocol — buy in this order:**
1. Dry goods (rice, cans, pasta)
2. Hardy vegetables (potato, onion, carrot)
3. Delicate fruit (strawberries, tomatoes)
4. **Meat and dairy — LAST. Straight into the rigid cooler.**

**Cold chain transport:** Cooler rides inside the car with A/C on.
Never in the trunk — it becomes an oven in Paraguay's heat.
Rule of 3 minutes: from car to client's fridge, under 3 minutes.

**Scale-up note (>40 portions/week):** Coordinate a shared bulk run
to Frigomas / FrigoChorti with Ivan or another large client → saves ~20% on protein.

---

### Friday — Mise en Place + Stocks (4.5 active hrs)

| Task | Active Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Butchery — break down whole animals, portion per dish | 1.5 hrs | |
| Full mise en place (vegetable cuts, marinades, rubs) | 2 hrs | |
| Bone broths + dark stock on low heat | Passive | Set and forget |
| Long braises (ragú 4 hrs, slow stews) | Passive + 0.5 hr supervision | Start Friday, finish Saturday |
| Intermediate clean | 0.5 hrs | |

**Total active Friday: ~4.5 hrs.** Passive cooking does not count.

---

### Saturday — Batch Cooking Day (10–12 hrs)

This is the heaviest day. With 1 assistant (cousin, IGA intern), drops to 8 hrs.

| Block | Activity |
|---|---|
| 9:00–11:00 | Finish Friday's braises and stews |
| 11:00–13:00 | Cook Option A (main — beef) |
| 13:00–14:00 | Cook Option B (poultry or pasta) |
| 14:00–15:00 | Cook Option C (vegetarian / soup) |
| 15:00–16:00 | Garnishes, final sauces, seasoning adjustments |
| 16:00–17:00 | **Crash cooling** — 90°C → 20°C in <20 min per batch |
| 17:00–19:30 | Vacuum seal + label every portion |
| 19:30–20:30 | Pack bags per client (thermal bags, ice packs) |
| 20:30–21:30 | Deep kitchen clean |

**Crash cooling method (mandatory — never skip):**
Hot pot → larger pot with ice water → stir 5 min.
Target: 90°C to 20°C in under 20 minutes.
Never put hot food directly into the freezer — it will ruin everything beside it.

**Label every portion with:**
- Dish name
- Production date
- Expiry date (7 days fridge / 30 days freezer)
- Reheating instructions (microwave / bain-marie / oven, 10–15 min)

---

## Menu & Rotation

### 4-Week Rotation (3 options + 1 extra per week)

| Week | Option A (beef) | Option B (poultry) | Option C (veg/soup) | Extra |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Boloñesa de autor | Pollo en pepitoria | Lentejas petit Paris | Crema de zapallo (jar) |
| **2** | Asado a la olla | Canelones de ave | Puchero de autor | Vori Vori (jar) |
| **3** | Goulash con spätzle | Pollo Grand-Mère | Minestrone | Caldo de huesos (jar) |
| **4** | Mandioca pie c/ragú | Ave desmechada | Lasagna pasta fresca | Plato del Chef |

12 distinct dishes per month. 25 dishes in the full catalog → no repeat for ~6 rotations (~2 months).

### Menu Design Rules (every week)

1. 1 red meat + 1 poultry + 1 vegetarian/soup — always.
2. At least 1 Paraguayan dish per month (pepitoria, puchero, vori vori, sopa paraguaya, etc.).
3. At least 1 option under Gs. 25,000 (accessible price point).
4. No repeat within the same 4-week block.
5. The "extra" rotates: cream soup, bone broth, jar soup, zero-waste chef's plate.

### Freezer Compatibility Criteria (to enter the catalog)

1. Survives 7 days fridge + 30 days freezer without quality loss.
2. Tolerates simple reheating (microwave / bain-marie / oven).
3. Cost per portion < Gs. 18,000 (allows Gs. 28,000 public price).
4. Scalable to 20+ portions from a home kitchen.

### Explicitly Excluded (freezer-incompatible)

Soufflés, omelets, gnocchi with crispy skin, brioche burgers, fresh tacos, baked pizzas,
fried empanadas, bruschettas, fried milanesas, crepes.

## Pricing Structure

### Price Tiers

| Tier | Condition | Discount |
|---|---|---|
| Public (general) | Order ≥ 3 portions/week | — |
| **Anchor Client** | ≥ 6 portions/week for 12 consecutive weeks | **−14%** |
| Family Pack | ≥ 4 portions of the same dish | −10% |
| First Week (promo) | New client, first week only | −15% |
| Referral bonus | Refer 1 new client | −10% next week |

### Prices per Portion (2026)

| Category | Public Price | Anchor Price |
|---|---|---|
| Premium beef (Asado, Goulash, Estofado, etc.) | Gs. 30,000–32,000 | Gs. 26,000–28,000 |
| Standard beef (Boloñesa, Albóndigas, Mandioca pie) | Gs. 28,000 | Gs. 24,000 |
| Poultry (Pepitoria, Grand-Mère, Canelones) | Gs. 24,000–30,000 | Gs. 20,000–26,000 |
| Vegetarian / legume / soup | Gs. 24,000–28,000 | Gs. 20,000–24,000 |
| Frascos (crema, caldo, sopa) — 250 ml | Gs. 18,000–22,000 | Gs. 14,000–18,000 |

**Sweet spot for public price: Gs. 28,000/portion.**
Above industrial (Gs. 20–30k), well below restaurant delivery (Gs. 55–85k).

### Payment Methods

| Method | When | Fee |
|---|---|---|

## Unit Economics

### Cost Per Portion (400 g average)

| Component | Gs. | % |
|---|---|---|
| Protein (~90 g cooked, whole-animal sourcing) | 6,500 | 44% |
| Carbohydrate (mandioca, rice, pasta, potato) | 1,500 | 10% |
| Vegetables | 2,000 | 13% |
| Pantry (oil, salt, spices, egg, cheese) | 1,500 | 10% |
| Gas + electricity + water | 800 | 5% |
| Packaging (vacuum bag + label) | 1,200 | 8% |
| Waste + adjustments (+10%) | 1,350 | 9% |
| **Total variable cost** | **14,850** | **100%** |

### Gross Margin by Category

| Price Point | Gross Margin | Dishes |
|---|---|---|
| Gs. 30,000–32,000 (premium) | 46–52% | Asado, Goulash, Estofado, Lasagna |
| Gs. 28,000 (standard) | 47–50% | Boloñesa, Albóndigas, Pepitoria |
| Gs. 24,000–26,000 (accessible) | 43–50% | Lentils, Minestrone, Vori Vori |
| Gs. 18,000–22,000 (jars) | 58–65% | Crema, Bone broth, Sopa |

**Catalog average at public price: ~47% gross margin.**
**At anchor client price: ~38% gross margin.**

## Cycle-Breaking Risks (and Fixes)

| Problem | Warning Signal | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Menu posted late (after Sun 21:00) | Orders drop 30% | Prepare copy + photos on Friday; Sunday just hits send |
| Too few orders | <10 portions by Wed 20:00 | Cancel the week with 48hr notice rather than cook at a loss |
| Late shopping (Abasto after 9am) | Stock gaps, no best cuts | Move protein order to Wednesday night; some stalls open |
| Delivery delayed | Client not home on Sunday | Sunday 9–12 is non-negotiable; Saturday night fallback with notice |
| Protein cost spike | Cost/portion > Gs. 9,000 | Gross margin drops from 47% to ~30%; adjust price or swap protein |
| Anchor client dropout | 1 of 3 anclas leaves | Revenue −25%, margin drops harder; replace within 2 weeks |

---

## Model Sensitivities

The business is **volume-sensitive**, not margin-sensitive. The #1 priority in the first 6 months
is **acquiring and retaining clients**, not refining recipes.

| Variable | Base Case | Break Point |
|---|---|---|
| Protein cost | Gs. 6,500/portion | If → Gs. 9,000: margin collapses to 30% |
| Weekly volume | 60 portions | If → 30: net margin halves |
| Anchor client retention | 0 dropouts | 1 dropout = −25% revenue |
| Packaging cost | Gs. 1,200/portion | +50% = −Gs. 36k/week at 60 portions |
